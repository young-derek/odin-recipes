Homemade Chipotle Burrito

Description

This is a unique take on Chipotle's vegetarian burrito from https://www.makebetterfood.com/recipes/homemade-chipotle-burrito/.

Ingredients

  • Black beans
    • 1 cup dried black beans
    • 1 teaspoon canola oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 3 cups of water
  • Cilantro-Lime Rice
    • 1 tablespoon canola oil
    • 1 cup white basmati rice
    • 2 cups water
    • squeezed juice from one lime
    • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
    • 2 teaspoons finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • Fajita Vegetables
    • 2 green bell peppers
    • 1 red onion
    • 1/2 tablespoon chopped fresh oregano
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Mild Tomato Salsa
    • 2 large, ripe tomatoes
    • 1 small green jalapeno, seeded and minced
    • 2 tablespoons red onion, minced
    • 1/2 tablespoon fresh squeezed lime juice
    • 1/2 tablespoon fresh squeezed lemon juice
    • 2 teaspoons finely chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Medium Roasted Chili-Corn Salsa
    • 2 cups frozen corn
    • 2 poblano chilis, finely minced
    • 1 small green jalapeno, seeded and minced
    • 2 tablespoons red onion, minced
    • 1/2 tablespoon fresh squeezed lime juice
    • 1/2 tablespoon fresh squeezed lemon juice
    • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Guacamole
    • 4 ripe avocados
    • 1 cup red onion, minced
    • 2 tablespoons fresh squeezed lime juice
    • 2 teaspoons coarse sea salt
    • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, to taste
    • 1 ripe tomato, seeds and pulp removed, chopped
  • Sour Cream
  • Cheese
    • 8 ounces monterey jack cheese, shredded
    • 8 ounces white cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 head of fresh romaine lettuce
  • 8 to 12 extra large soft tortillas

Steps

  1. Black Beans
    1. Rinse beans and place in a large saucepan. Cover with water 2 inches higher than beans and soak overnight.
    2. Drain beans in a colander and rinse. Return to saucepan or stock pot.
    3. Add water and bring to a boil.
    4. Add oil, cumin and cilantro to pan with black beans. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until beans are fully cooked and flavors have combined, about 90 minutes.
    5. Remove from heat. Find and remove bay leaf and drain excess water. Set aside to serve.
  2. Cilantro-Lime Rice
    1. In a 2-quart heavy saucepan, heat oil over low heat, stirring occasionally until melted.
    2. Add rice and lime juice to pot. Cook until rice is almost translucent.
    3. Add water and salt to pot and bring to a hard boil.
    4. Once water boils, cover and reduce heat to low. Simmer and cook undisturbed until rice is tender and water is fully absorbed, about 15-20 minutes.
    5. Turn off the heat and leave pot covered for 30 minutes to allow rice to steam. Do not remove cover or stir rice.
    6. Transfer rice to a medium bowl and fluff with a fork.
    7. In a small bowl or ramekin, mix lime juice and salt together. Pour over rice and stir to combine.
    8. Add cilantro to rice and stir to combine.
  3. Fajita Vegetables
    1. Chop vegetables and oregano and toss together in a small bowl.
    2. Heat oil in a medium skillet over medium-high until simmering.
    3. Saute vegetables until fragrant and starting to brown on edges slightly, about 5 minutes.
    4. Remove from heat and reserve in bowl.
  4. Mild Tomato Salsa
    1. Core and seed tomatoes. Dice into small pieces and place in a medium bowl.
    2. Mince jalapeno and onion. Add to bowl with tomatoes.
    3. Add juices, cilantro, and salt to bowl with tomato mixture. Stir to combine.
  5. Medium Roasted Chili-Corn Salsa
    1. Cook corn in a small saucepan. Drain and reserve in a medium bowl.
    2. Mince poblano chillies and cook in now-empty sauce pan over medium-high heat until fragrant, about 3 minutes.
    3. Remove from heat and add to bowl with corn.
    4. Add juices, cilantro, and salt to bowl with corn mixture. Stir to combine.
  6. Guacamole
    1. Using a knife, cut avocados in half and remove pits. Using spoon, scoop out green flesh into a medium sized non-reactive bowl. Using a potato masher (or a fork), mash the avocados.
    2. Add chopped onion, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix with a spatula until well combined.
    3. Chop tomato. If serving immediately, mix in with guacamole. Otherwise, store tomatoes in an air tight container and mix into guacamole right before serving.
  7. Sour Cream
    1. Place sour cream in a small bowl for serving on burritos.
  8. Cheese
    1. Shred cheese and mix together in a medium bowl.
  9. Lettuce
    1. Wash several pieces of fresh romaine lettuce. Dry each leaf.
    2. Roll each leaf up tightly. Using a sharp knife, cut rolled up leaf into 1/4-inch pieces, creating long, thin strands of shredded lettuce. Reserve in a bowl.
  10. Burrito Assembly
    1. Top tortilla with your favourite mix of homemade Chipotle-style toppings.
    2. To roll, fold in sides of tortilla over rice, fold tortilla edge closest to you over toppings, and pull tortilla edge farthest from you down, tightening wrap as you go. It takes some practice, so have a fork handy for the first few burritos while you get the hang of it.
